# Thin Topic Balance Expansion Design

## Purpose

Strengthen the portal by concentrating the next content expansion on the comparatively thin topic shelves rather than continuing to deepen the already-dense theory and WTO clusters.

The goal is not just to increase record counts. It is to improve the balance, usability, and comparative research value of the site by making weaker shelves feel like real sub-corpora with mixed material types, stronger institutional coverage, and better propagation through actor, institution, and dimension pages.

## Approved Product Direction

The user approved the following direction:

- concentrate on the thinner topics first
- use a balance-first expansion rather than a fixed equal-per-topic count across the whole site
- keep the work inside the current research-portal structure rather than redesigning the information architecture again

This means the next batch should strengthen the shelves that are currently underdeveloped relative to the rest of the site.

## Current Shelf Imbalance

At the start of this design pass, the topic counts are roughly:

- `united-states`: 12
- `china`: 13
- `international-investment`: 13
- `monetary-financial-regulation`: 15
- `ai-governance`: 15
- `european-union`: 17
- `middle-small-powers`: 18
- `cyber-data-governance`: 24
- `digital-trade-ecommerce`: 37
- `wto-reform`: 38
- `great-powers`: 49
- `theories-rulemaking`: 85

The imbalance is not only numerical. The thin shelves also differ in internal composition:

- `united-states` is official-text heavy and underdeveloped in scholarship and multilateral-facing materials
- `china` is strong on official laws and agreements but thin on reports, institutional practice, and analytical materials
- `international-investment` has a useful institutional base but needs more reform, facilitation, and comparative literature
- `monetary-financial-regulation` is dominated by institutional and treaty-style materials and needs more analytical and crisis-governance coverage
- `ai-governance` already has some official texts but still lacks a strong literature and governance-competition layer

## Target Topics

This expansion pass should focus primarily on five topics:

- `united-states`
- `china`
- `international-investment`
- `monetary-financial-regulation`
- `ai-governance`

These are the primary destinations for the next content batch. Other topics may receive indirect benefits through cross-linking, but they are not the sourcing priority for this pass.

## Core Approach

Use a single balance-first content batch that deliberately improves the weakest shelves without forcing artificial symmetry.

This means:

- add materials where they most improve the thin shelves
- prefer mixed-source and mixed-record-type additions over repetitive document stacking
- link each new record beyond its home shelf when that improves retrieval through actors, institutions, dimensions, and related topics
- keep the portal structure stable and let the new value come from better records and better relationships

The batch should be substantial enough to change the shape of the weak shelves, but not so large that quality control collapses.

## Batch Shape

The recommended batch size is approximately `25-30` new records total.

Working target by shelf:

- `united-states`: `4-6` records
- `china`: `4-6` records
- `international-investment`: `4-6` records
- `monetary-financial-regulation`: `4-6` records
- `ai-governance`: `4-6` records

These are guidance targets, not rigid quotas. The real constraint is fit and quality.

If one shelf yields five strong additions and another yields four, that is preferable to forcing weak filler to hit an exact number.

## Shelf-Specific Content Priorities

### United States

Add materials that show US rule-making through legal design, bargaining leadership, standards, market access, and domestic-law externalization.

Priority material types:

- official strategy and policy texts
- model instruments and negotiating templates
- institutional leadership documents
- analytical literature on US external regulatory power
- selected report-style materials where they clarify US rule export

Examples of likely fit:

- USTR, State Department, Commerce, Treasury, NIST, White House, or congressional materials
- literature on the United States as a rule-maker through trade, finance, sanctions, export controls, and standards

### China

Add materials that show Chinese rule-making not only through domestic law, but also through institutional reform proposals, development finance, standards, digital governance, and alternative or parallel institutional practice.

Priority material types:

- official statements and policy texts
- institutional and development-finance documents
- research reports and analytical materials
- selected scholarship that clarifies Chinese rule-making style or capacity

Examples of likely fit:

- reform proposals
- BRI, development finance, standards, digital-governance, or AI-governance materials
- reports from authoritative policy institutions where they illuminate practice

### International Investment

Add materials that rebalance the shelf toward current rule redesign rather than only classic treaty protection or dispute settlement anchors.

Priority material types:

- UNCITRAL, ICSID, OECD, UNCTAD, or World Bank documents
- investment facilitation and screening materials
- ISDS reform texts
- comparative literature on rebalancing, facilitation, and regulatory space

Examples of likely fit:

- reform process documents
- interpretive or model-text materials
- literature on treaty redesign, screening, facilitation, and reform politics

### Monetary and Financial Regulation

Add materials that show who makes the rules in crises, standard-setting, macro-financial governance, payments, and financial stability.

Priority material types:

- IMF, BIS, Basel, FSB, IOSCO, CPMI, or World Bank documents
- crisis-governance and reform statements
- analytical literature on transgovernmental or expert-led rule-making
- selected historical or institutional texts where they deepen the shelf

Examples of likely fit:

- capital flow, financial stability, payments, sovereign debt, or post-crisis reform materials
- literature on regulatory coordination and expert authority

### AI Governance

Add materials that make the shelf read as a true governance field rather than a short set of official texts.

Priority material types:

- intergovernmental and summit materials
- standards or framework documents
- institutional-competition reports
- comparative scholarship on AI governance models and rule-setting

Examples of likely fit:

- UN, OECD, G7, Council of Europe, standards-related, or national-framework materials
- literature on governance rivalry, model export, institutional choice, or standard-setting

## Record-Type and Source-Mix Rules

This batch should improve internal variety within each thin shelf.

Rules:

- each thin shelf should gain at least two different record types in this batch
- the overall batch should add more literature to `united-states` and `ai-governance`
- the overall batch should add more reports and institutional materials to `china`
- the overall batch should add more reform-oriented institutional and literature items to `international-investment`
- the overall batch should add more analytical literature and official statements to `monetary-financial-regulation`

Source handling should remain conservative:

- use official or institutionally authoritative sources first for legal texts, reports, and formal documents
- use metadata-only scholarship records when full text should not be reproduced
- preserve explicit language-status labeling when multiple language variants exist

## Linking and Propagation Rules

Every new record must be treated as part of the whole portal, not just as an isolated topic entry.

Rules:

- each new record must have one clear home topic among the five thin shelves
- add cross-topic links only when they materially improve retrieval
- review every new record for `actors`, `institutions`, and `dimensions`
- use cross-links aggressively where they are analytically justified, especially to:
  - `great-powers`
  - `middle-small-powers`
  - `cyber-data-governance`
  - `digital-trade-ecommerce`
  - `wto-reform`

Institutional propagation matters in this batch because several weak shelves are institution-thin or institution-skewed. Underused but important institutions should be linked where the record supports them, especially:

- `imf`
- `fsb`
- `iosco`
- `cpmi`
- `unctad`
- `uncitral`
- `icsid`
- `un`
- `oecd`
- relevant US and Chinese institutional nodes already present in the portal

## Quality Rules

The batch should follow strict quality control.

Rules:

- do not add a record unless it clearly strengthens one of the thin shelves
- do not use filler just to hit a numeric target
- prefer analytically meaningful breadth over repetitive versions of the same instrument
- avoid duplicating existing records or near-duplicates
- do not weaken source hierarchy in order to add volume quickly

This pass is successful only if the thin shelves become visibly better research shelves, not simply longer lists.

## Testing and Validation

This expansion should be locked with both data checks and rendered review.

Data validation should include:

- stronger minimum shelf expectations for the five thin topics
- at least one expectation that each strengthened shelf retains mixed record types
- continued validation of resolved topic, actor, institution, and dimension links

Visual validation should include:

- the updated topic pages for the thin shelves
- at least one representative new record page from the batch
- external Chrome review rather than relying only on the in-app browser

## Non-Goals

This batch should not:

- redesign the site structure again
- re-open the dimensions architecture discussion
- bulk-expand already-dense shelves such as `theories-rulemaking` unless cross-linking makes that unavoidable
- prioritize raw quantity over fit

## Success Criteria

The balance-first expansion is successful when:

- the five thin shelves each gain meaningful new depth
- the added materials improve type diversity and institutional coverage
- the new records propagate usefully into actor, institution, database, and dimension views
- the site feels less theory-heavy and more even across substantive research areas
- the portal remains clean, structured, and source-disciplined while becoming more useful for comparative rule-making research
