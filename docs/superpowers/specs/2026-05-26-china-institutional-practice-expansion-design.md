# China Institutional Practice Expansion Design

## Purpose

Strengthen the portal by making the next major expansion a China-first institutional-practice dossier rather than another mixed general batch.

The site already has a usable China shelf, but it is still skewed toward domestic laws and high-level initiatives. The next pass should show China as an actor operating across the rule-making venues of the international economic system, including trade, finance, development, digital governance, and coalition forums.

This batch should materially improve the monograph- and article-building value of the site by making China's policy, performance, and practices easier to trace across institutions.

## Approved Product Direction

The user approved the following direction:

- use a dedicated China institutional-practice expansion as the next major content batch
- prioritize official and authoritative materials on China's participation in international organizations and rule-making forums
- ensure China-related records appear first on the China page and are also surfaced prominently on other relevant pages
- keep the existing Topic Atlas + Database architecture rather than redesigning the site

This means the next expansion should focus on structured retrieval and institutional coverage, not on changing the front-end information architecture.

## Core Expansion Shape

The next expansion should be a dedicated China institutional-practice dossier of approximately `35` new records.

The batch should be organized across four forum clusters:

- `WTO`
- `IMF / World Bank / AIIB / NDB`
- `UN / UNCTAD / UNESCAP`
- `G20 / APEC / BRICS / BRI`

The goal is not merely to increase the number of China-tagged records. The goal is to make the China shelf function as a serious research dossier on how China uses, contests, reforms, and builds rule-making venues in the international economic system.

## Why This Batch Is Needed

At the start of this design pass, the current corpus shows a clear imbalance in the China shelf:

- the China topic has only a modest number of linked records relative to the overall corpus
- China-linked materials are concentrated in domestic laws, top-level initiatives, and a small number of official texts
- China's participation through `WTO`, `IMF`, `World Bank`, `UN`, `UNCTAD`, `G20`, and `APEC` is still thin
- the China topic does not yet read like a full institutional-practice shelf

This creates a research gap. The portal can already show what China legislates domestically, but it is weaker at showing what China says, proposes, negotiates, builds, and supports across international forums.

## Sourcing Approach

The approved sourcing model is institution-first and official-source-led.

The batch should be built by forum cluster:

- `WTO`
- `IMF / World Bank / AIIB / NDB`
- `UN / UNCTAD / UNESCAP`
- `G20 / APEC / BRICS / BRI`

Within each cluster, official Chinese and institutional documents should form the spine of the batch. A thinner layer of scholarship, analytical reports, or authoritative commentary may be added where it clarifies institutional practice, significance, or comparative implications.

This approach was chosen over chronology-first or document-type-first sourcing because it aligns best with the current portal architecture and directly strengthens institution pages, topic pages, and the China shelf at the same time.

## Scope of Materials

The batch should include materials concerning China's relevant policy, performance, and practices in international economic rule-making.

Priority material categories include:

- official statements
- communications and submissions
- reform proposals
- negotiation records
- institutional or strategy documents
- leadership-linked initiatives
- communiques and declarations
- governance or development-finance framework texts
- authoritative reports on implementation or participation
- selected scholarship and books or chapters that interpret Chinese practice

The batch should not be limited to journal articles and books. Official and quasi-official practice materials are a core part of the intended shelf.

Examples of fit include:

- Chinese submissions or statements in WTO reform, e-commerce, or investment facilitation processes
- China-linked IMF, World Bank, AIIB, or NDB governance and participation texts
- UN and UNCTAD-facing development, digital governance, or investment-rule materials
- G20, APEC, BRICS, and BRI documents that show coalition-building, agenda-setting, or institutional entrepreneurship

## Record Model Strategy

The expansion should stay within the current structured record architecture.

Implementation rules:

- add the new materials in one dedicated China-focused batch file
- append that batch through the existing record aggregator
- do not create a new top-level route or separate China-only data model
- enrich institution data only when accurate tagging requires a missing institution node

This keeps the site coherent and avoids fragmenting the corpus across too many data entry points.

## Coverage Rules

Each record in the new batch should answer a concrete research question:

What did China say, propose, build, support, or do in this forum that affected international economic rule-making?

This requirement is meant to prevent drift into generic China coverage or low-value news accumulation.

Hard coverage rules:

- at least `4` forum clusters must be represented:
  - `WTO`
  - `IMF / World Bank / AIIB / NDB`
  - `UN / UNCTAD / UNESCAP`
  - `G20 / APEC / BRICS / BRI`
- at least `5` record types must be represented overall
- the preferred record types are:
  - `official-statement`
  - `institutional-document`
  - `negotiation-record`
  - `treaty-agreement`
  - `research-report`
- every new record must link to `china`
- every new record must also connect to at least one broader retrieval surface:
  - a relevant institution page
  - a second topic such as `wto-reform`, `international-investment`, `monetary-financial-regulation`, `digital-trade-ecommerce`, `ai-governance`, or `middle-small-powers`
  - `great-powers` where analytically justified

Scholarship should be thinner than the official corpus in this pass and should mainly be used to interpret practice rather than replace primary materials.

## Forum Cluster Priorities

### WTO

The WTO cluster should focus on China's role in institutional reform, negotiations, and issue-specific rule-making.

Priority materials include:

- reform proposals
- statements and submissions
- e-commerce and digital trade participation records
- investment facilitation materials
- ministerial or negotiation-facing documents

The purpose is to show China not only as a member state, but as a participant in shaping the negotiation and reform agenda from within the WTO.

### IMF / World Bank / AIIB / NDB

This cluster should show China's participation through monetary governance, development finance, institutional design, and reform discourse.

Priority materials include:

- founding or governance texts where China is central
- speeches and policy framing on reform and development finance
- institutional outputs that reveal China's participation or influence
- authoritative reports or statements relevant to development, investment, or financial governance

This cluster should help the portal show China as both participant in existing Bretton Woods institutions and builder of complementary or alternative development-finance institutions.

### UN / UNCTAD / UNESCAP

This cluster should cover China's participation in economic-governance and development-rule processes within the UN system.

Priority materials include:

- UN-facing statements on development and digital governance
- UNCTAD or UNESCAP materials involving Chinese participation, initiative framing, or implementation pathways
- documents that show coalition-building, agenda-setting, or policy positioning in multilateral settings

The point is not to turn the site into a general UN archive. The point is to capture rule-making-relevant economic and development governance materials.

### G20 / APEC / BRICS / BRI

This cluster should show China's role in coalition forums and initiative-led governance.

Priority materials include:

- summit outputs
- declarations and concept notes
- chair or participation statements
- implementation documents linked to digital economy, finance, development, or connectivity governance
- coalition-building materials in BRICS, APEC, G20, and BRI settings

This cluster is important because much of China's practical rule-shaping activity occurs through coordinated or semi-institutional forums rather than through formal treaty systems alone.

## China-First Retrieval and Ordering

The user required China-related records to appear first on the dedicated China page and to surface prominently on other relevant pages.

This should become an explicit retrieval rule.

Behavior rules:

- on the dedicated `China` topic page, records tagged with `china` are the primary shelf and appear first by default
- on other relevant topic pages such as `WTO Reform`, `International Investment`, `Monetary and Financial Regulation`, `Digital Trade and E-Commerce`, and `AI Governance`, records matching the page and tagged `china` should be promoted ahead of non-China records
- on relevant institution pages, China-linked records should also be promoted ahead of more generic records when they match that institution

Recommended ranking order for mixed pages:

1. records matching the page's main topic or institution and tagged `china`
2. other strongly relevant records for that page
3. broader contextual or comparative records

Where a page is especially mixed, the UI may make this visible through a short lead grouping such as `China in this forum` ahead of the fuller record list.

This rule should make China-facing materials immediately visible without removing the comparative architecture of the broader site.

## Institutional Propagation Rules

The batch should improve not only the China topic page, but also the institution pages and secondary topic pages.

China-linked records should materially strengthen the presence of China on:

- `WTO`
- `IMF`
- `World Bank`
- `UN`
- `UNCTAD`
- `G20`
- `APEC`

Additional institution nodes such as `AIIB`, `NDB`, or `BRICS` may be added if needed for accurate tagging and retrieval.

The point of this propagation is to let researchers find China's practice from multiple directions:

- by actor
- by topic
- by institution
- by comparative rule-making context

## Source Hierarchy and Language Handling

The batch should remain strict about source quality.

Rules:

- use official or otherwise authoritative sources first
- where both Chinese and English variants exist, keep language status explicit
- do not present unofficial translations as authoritative
- use news items only when they document actual participation, initiative launch, institutional positioning, or concrete governance practice
- avoid generic media coverage that does not advance the research value of the portal

This rule preserves the site's public research quality and keeps the China shelf useful for later monograph and article drafting.

## Testing and Validation

This expansion should be locked with both data validation and retrieval validation.

Required validation themes:

- the China topic shelf should rise materially in record count and record-type variety
- institution pages should show stronger China-linked presence, especially for `WTO`, `IMF`, `World Bank`, `UN`, `UNCTAD`, `G20`, and `APEC`
- ordering logic should confirm that China-tagged records surface first on the China page and are promoted on related topic and institution pages
- new records should continue to satisfy schema, source, topic, actor, institution, and dimension checks
- language-status handling should remain explicit where records use both Chinese and English versions

Visual or rendered review should inspect:

- the dedicated China page
- at least several related topic pages
- at least several institution pages with strong China-facing materials
- representative record pages from different forum clusters

## Non-Goals

This batch should not:

- redesign the current portal architecture
- turn the site into a generic China policy archive
- prioritize raw quantity over institutionally meaningful fit
- replace official materials with commentary when primary materials are available
- bury China-facing materials inside mixed lists without retrieval prioritization

## Success Criteria

This batch is successful when:

- the China topic becomes a visibly denser and more serious institutional-practice shelf
- the portal no longer treats China mainly as a shelf of domestic laws
- China's rule-making activity is legible across trade, finance, development, digital governance, standards, and coalition forums
- China-linked materials become easy to discover from the China page and from other relevant topic and institution pages
- the new batch supports sustained use for monograph drafting and article-level comparative research
